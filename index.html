HAWAII and Sustainability

This study abroad trip was very eye-opening for me. Not only was going to Hawai’i an amazing adventure and super fun, but I also learned how to make progress in my personal life (me in HI) as well as environmentally which is very important.

We need to preserve our planet for future generations and we can do this by progressing to a greener outlook on life

Progress is the onward movement towards a destination.

So what route can I take to improve my personal progress and how do I plan to contribute to the progression of sustainability?

“A journey of a 1000 miles starts with one step.”

This was my first step towards progress. It was a leap of faith for me to participate in this study abroad as I had to step out of my comfort zone.

And I’m glad I did because: I met some fantastic people and made new friends. I learned many interesting facts about a culture and their traditions that are very different from mine. I realized how fragile our planet is.

With this realization, I learned that it starts with one person, me, myself and I to be able to move mountains, cross oceans, and battle red hot lava to achieve sustainability.

We can do this by maintaining the environment for our future generations.

There are small things we can do to ensure the survival of plants and animals Including:

  • Leaving only footprints and taking only photos everywhere I go
  • Using reusable water bottles
  • Participating in service projects

Since this trip, I have been constantly reminding my friends and family how important it is to achieve sustainability. All thanks to this program and Hawaii!

I would call that progress, wouldn’t you?

I will never forget this experience!

It taught me how important sustainability truly is and I now take that into consideration in my daily life. As far as personal progress I learned that it is worth it to take that leap as you never know the exciting places it might take you!

Hey, it might even take you to Hawaii!
